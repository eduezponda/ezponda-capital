## The Thesis in One Paragraph

Copper is the metal that makes the modern world move. Every EV, every data center, every wind turbine needs it, and there's no substitute. Freeport-McMoRan controls Grasberg, a deposit in Papua, Indonesia so large and so rich that it has no geological peer. While the market prices FCX as a cyclical miner, we believe it deserves to trade as a strategic infrastructure asset: irreplaceable supply, growing demand, and a management team that has navigated every cycle for 30 years.

---

## Why Copper, Why Now

The structural demand case for copper has never been stronger. It is not driven by one trend but by three concurrent megatrends colliding simultaneously:

**Electrification of transport.** A conventional car uses roughly 23 kg of copper. An EV uses 83 kg. As the global fleet electrifies, the incremental demand per vehicle is ~60 kg, multiplied across tens of millions of units per year.

**Power grid buildout.** Grids built in the 20th century were designed for centralized fossil fuel generation. The transition to distributed renewables requires complete rewiring: new transmission lines, substations, offshore wind interconnects. The IEA estimates grid investment must triple by 2030.

**AI infrastructure.** This is the underappreciated driver. A hyperscale AI data center requires 4–6× more power infrastructure per square foot than a traditional data center. Cooling systems, backup power, and dense compute clusters are all copper-intensive. Every GPU cluster being deployed today has a copper supply chain upstream.

On the supply side, the picture is structurally constrained. Average ore grades at existing mines are declining ~1.5% per year. New greenfield discoveries are rare, remote, and take 15–20 years from discovery to production. The pipeline of committed new supply cannot close the gap the market is pricing in.

## Why Freeport-McMoRan

**Grasberg is the asset.** Located in the Sudirman Mountains of Papua, Grasberg is the world's largest copper mine and the second largest gold mine. The ore grades are approximately 3× the industry average. Underground expansion at the Deep Mill Level Zone (DMLZ) and Grasberg Block Cave (GBC) is ramping production toward a multi-decade plateau.

**Scale and diversification.** Beyond Grasberg, FCX operates major assets in the Americas: Morenci (Arizona), Cerro Verde (Peru), and El Abra (Chile). These provide geographic diversification and a lower-cost base that supports the balance sheet through copper price cycles.

**Leverage to the copper price.** Every $0.10/lb move in the copper price translates to approximately $350–400M in annual EBITDA for FCX at current production volumes. At $4.50/lb copper, FCX generates significant free cash flow. At $5.00–$5.50/lb, a range many analysts view as the long-run equilibrium needed to incentivize new supply, FCX's earnings power is transformational.

**Management and capital allocation.** Richard Adkerson has led FCX for over two decades. The company navigated the 2015–2016 commodity downturn with aggressive debt reduction, and has since rebuilt a fortress balance sheet. The current capital return framework of dividends plus variable returns reflects a management team that understands capital cycle discipline.

## Key Risks

No thesis is complete without a rigorous accounting of what can go wrong.

**Indonesia/geopolitical risk.** The Grasberg operating agreement with the Indonesian government (PT-FI) has been renegotiated multiple times. Any future renegotiation, export restriction, or nationalization pressure could impair the asset's value. This is the single largest non-market risk.

**Copper price cyclicality.** FCX is not immune to the commodity cycle. A global recession, particularly one led by Chinese construction weakness, could compress copper prices toward $3.00/lb, meaningfully reducing free cash flow and potentially pressuring the dividend.

**Execution on underground ramp.** Transitioning Grasberg from open-pit to underground block cave mining is one of the most complex engineering undertakings in the mining industry. Delays, safety incidents, or geotechnical challenges could impair near-term production and increase costs.

**Currency and cost inflation.** FCX's cost base is sensitive to energy prices, labor costs in Indonesia and Peru, and the strength of the US dollar. Sustained cost inflation could compress margins even at healthy copper prices.

## Valuation Framework

We do not anchor to a single price target, but rather think about FCX across three scenarios:

| Scenario | Copper Price | FCX EBITDA | Multiple | Implied Value |
|----------|-------------|------------|---------|--------------|
| Bear | $3.50/lb | ~$6B | 6× | ~$18/share |
| Base | $4.50/lb | ~$10B | 7× | ~$32/share |
| Bull | $5.50/lb | ~$15B | 8× | ~$54/share |

*Note: Figures are illustrative estimates for framework purposes, not financial projections.*

The asymmetry is notable: in a bear case, downside is meaningful but bounded by asset value. In a bull case, which we view as increasingly probable given structural demand, the upside is substantial. The option value embedded in Grasberg's long reserve life is not captured in near-term earnings models.

The following indicators determine whether the thesis is on track:

- **Copper price trend:** Are we tracking toward the $4.50–$5.50/lb range?
- **Grasberg underground ramp:** Are quarterly production volumes at DMLZ and GBC meeting guidance?
- **Indonesian regulatory environment:** Any news on permit extensions, export policy, or government ownership discussions?
- **AI/data center capex:** Hyperscaler capex announcements as a leading indicator of copper demand.
- **FCX balance sheet:** Net debt trajectory and capital return announcements.
- **China demand:** Property sector stabilization is a key swing factor for near-term copper demand.

## Bottom Line

Freeport-McMoRan is not a trade on copper. It is a multi-decade position in an irreplaceable asset at the center of the most important infrastructure buildout of the 21st century. The risks are real and must be sized accordingly. But for investors with a 3–5 year horizon and the patience to hold through volatility, FCX offers a rare combination: quality asset, proven management, and structural demand tailwinds that are just beginning to accelerate.
